MUMBAI: The year 2015 might just become a year of exits for private equity and venture capital investors. In what is considered a biggest sign in the investor community, investors have finally started getting exits from their Indian investments. For the first time, the private equity and venture capital investors have managed to exit investments worth $6 billion or Rs 38,000 crore in the first seven months of the year between January and July, a Venture Intelligence report said Wednesday. \"This seven month figure is 33 per cent higher than the $4.5 billion realized by investors in the previous best (full) years of 2010 and 2005,\" the Venture Intelligence study said. Industry trackers say that the healthy exit figures also give confidence for investors to pump in more money in the country. According to the report the companies in the IT & ITES, BFSI, telecom, media and entertainment industries have led the list of successful exits in 2015 returning $2.4 billion, $1 billion, $770 million and $280 million respectively to investors. \"IT & ITES companies have provided a series of healthy exits to international PE investors during 2015. The acquisition of IT Services firm iGate by Capgemini for $4 billion enabled Apax Partners take home $1.3 billion or an about 3 times (\"3x\") return on its 2011 investment. ATM management services firm CMS Info Systems witnessed majority stakeholder Blackstone trade its holdings to incoming PE investor Baring Asia for about $250 million. Internet & Mobile Services focused Tiger Global tasted its first significant exit in the Indian market by completing the sale of its shares in publicly listed local search firm Justdial realizing a 13x return on its investment,\" the report added.
