The body of the article is as follows:\n    \nThe Culinary Institute of America,\nwhich Life Magazine called \u201cthe Harvard of Haute Cuisine,\u201d\nwants investors in the $3.7 trillion municipal market to help\nexpand a student center, kitchens and a dining hall in upstate\n New York .  CIA, based in Hyde Park, was founded in  Connecticut  in 1946\nto train returning World War II veterans and offers bachelor\u2019s\ndegrees as well as courses for food enthusiasts, according to\nthe school\u2019s website. It plans to borrow $31 million through the\nDormitory Authority of the State of New York tomorrow even as\ndebt with similar ratings trails top-ranked securities by the\nmost since 2008.  The deal is one of the $2.4 billion of long-term issues\nlocalities plan this week, down from $4.2 billion in the\nprevious one, as the Labor Day holiday limits trading.\nGovernments are borrowing with benchmark muni yields at about\nthe highest level since April 2011 after individuals pulled $23\nbillion from local-debt mutual funds during the past 14 weeks,\nLipper US Fund Flows data show.  Among the school\u2019s graduates are author and television host\nAnthony Bourdain;  Chipotle Mexican Grill Inc. (CMG)  founder Steve Ells; and  Grant Achatz , the James Beard Outstanding Chef award\nwinner. The institute has seen enrollment rise by 3 percent\nsince 2009 and about 2,900 students will take classes this\nacademic year, according to deal documents.  \u2018Expanded Resources\u2019  \u201cDue to growth, we need to expand our dining-hall space,\u201d\nCharlie O\u2019Mara, senior vice president of finance and\nadministration, said by telephone. \u201cWe are attempting to\nprovide expanded resources for student recreation.\u201d  Proceeds will go toward a $26.1 million project to build a\n33,000 square-foot (3,000 square-meter) addition, with three\nkitchens and a self-serve cafeteria that holds 450 people,\nO\u2019Mara said. It replaces a 60-seat facility and is designed to\nserve students, he said. The borrowing also will help finish a\n$20.4 million hotel and conference center, he said.  The institute, which Life compared to  Harvard University  in\n1979, has $101.4 million of long-term debt, according to bond\ndocuments. This week\u2019s sale will be the last for at least two\nyears, O\u2019Mara said. The deal, which has a final maturity of\n2043, will price tomorrow, he said.  Moody\u2019s Investors Service ranked the debt Baa2, two steps\nabove junk. It cited the school\u2019s \u201cprudent fiscal management\u201d\nand its \u201chealthy growth\u201d in tuition. Still, it\u2019s vulnerable to\ndrops in enrollment because of reliance on such student charges,\nwhich account for 74 percent of total revenue in fiscal 2013,\nthe company said.  BBB rated securities, the same rank as Baa2, have lost 9.3\npercent this year through Aug. 29, compared with a 3.81 percent\ndecline for top-rated bonds, according to Bank of America\nMerrill Lynch indexes.  Other deals on tap this week include  Texas A&M University \nand the sewer system serving  Portland ,  Oregon .
