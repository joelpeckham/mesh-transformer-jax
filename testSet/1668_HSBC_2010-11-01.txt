The body of the article is as follows:\n    \nIndia\u2019s stocks rose, driving the\nbenchmark index to the highest level in two weeks, as surging\ncorporate earnings boosted the outlook for economic growth.  Sun Pharmaceutical Industries Ltd. , the nation\u2019s biggest\ndrugmaker by market value, gained to a record after its profit\nclimbed 12 percent on higher demand for generic medicines.\n National Aluminium Co. , the second-biggest producer of the\nmetal, advanced 0.9 percent after it posted a 41 percent\nincrease in net income.  Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd. , the largest\nmaker of sport-utility vehicles and tractors, rose to the\nhighest in at least 19 years after sales climbed in October.  \u201cThe results have largely been as per our expectations,\u201d\nsaid  Swati Kulkarni , a fund manager at UTI Asset Management Co.\nin Mumbai., which manages about $15.2 billion in assets. \u201cThe\ncontinuing of growth is a heartening sign.\u201d The high share\nvaluations needed the \u201cgrowth support\u201d and the companies have\n\u201cdelivered,\u201d she said.  The Bombay Stock Exchange\u2019s Sensitive Index, or  Sensex ,\ngained 323.29, or 1.6 percent, to 20,355.63, the highest level\nsince Oct. 14, as of the 3:30 p.m. close in Mumbai. The gauge\nhas gained 27 percent from a May 25 low and is the best\nperformer this year among the world\u2019s 10 biggest stock markets.\nForeign investment into local stocks has risen 73 percent this\nyear, making the Sensex the most expensive benchmark in Asia.  The S&P CNX  Nifty  Index on the National Stock Exchange rose\n1.7 percent to 6,117.55. The  BSE 200 Index  increased 1.7 percent\nto 2,583.69.  Asian Stocks  Asian  stocks  advanced after China\u2019s manufacturing expanded\nat the fastest pace in six months. \u201cThe positive mood in Asia\nafter China\u2019s manufacturing data has also added to the\nsentiments here,\u201d said  K.K. Mital , a New Delhi-based portfolio\nmanager with Globe Capital Market Ltd.  Sun Pharmaceutical increased 3.8 percent to 2,190.7 rupees,\nits highest level since the shares were listed in December 1994.\nIts profit rose to 5.04 billion rupees in the three months ended\nSept. 30, beating the 3.66 billion-rupee average of 17 analyst\nestimates compiled by Bloomberg.  National Aluminium climbed 0.9 percent to 405 rupees as net\nincome surged to 2.24 billion rupees in the three months ended\nSept. 30, aided by higher prices and production of the metal.  ICICI Bank , the country\u2019s second-biggest lender, soared 5.9\npercent to 1,232, its highest close since January 2008. The\nstock extended its 6.8 percent climb on Oct. 29 after quarterly\nprofit rose 19 percent.  Sterlite Industries (India) Ltd. , the largest copper and\nzinc producer, gained 1.3 percent to 170.85 rupees. Copper\nadvanced from London to Shanghai as investors sought\nalternatives to a declining dollar and as manufacturing\naccelerated in China, the world\u2019s largest user of industrial\nmetals.  Hindalco Industries  Hindalco Industries Ltd. , the biggest aluminum producer,\nincreased 1.2 percent to 212.95 rupees. Aluminum in London\ngained 0.8 percent.  Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd. , largest maker of sport-utility\nvehicles and tractors, jumped 4.5 percent to 766.5 rupees, its\nhighest in at least 19 years after its vehicle sales rose 34\npercent in October.  Tata Motors Ltd. , the biggest truckmaker and\nowner of Jaguar Land Rover, gained 1 percent to 1,170.6 rupees\nafter its October sales grew 21 percent.  The International Monetary Fund on Oct. 6 raised its 2010\neconomic growth forecast for India to 9.7 percent from 9.4\npercent, the fastest pace after China.  India\u2019s manufacturing growth accelerated in October. The\nPurchasing Managers\u2019 Index rose to 57.2 from 55.1 in September,\nHSBC Holdings Plc and Markit Economics said in an emailed\nstatement today. A reading above 50 indicates expansion.  Interest Rates  India\u2019s central bank may raise interest rates for a sixth\ntime this year tomorrow, according to 15 of 23 economists\u2019\nforecasts in a Bloomberg survey. Six of the eight predicting no\nincrease expect higher borrowing costs by yearend.  Trading was halted at the Bombay Stock Exchange, Asia\u2019s\noldest, from noon to 2:30 p.m. local time to resolve \u201ctechnical\nissues\u201d involving \u201ctrade confirmation,\u201d Deputy Chief\nExecutive Officer Ashishkumar Chauhan said.
