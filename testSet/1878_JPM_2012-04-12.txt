The body of the article is as follows:\n    \nChina\u2019s stocks  rose, driving the\nbenchmark index to its biggest gain in two months, amid\nspeculation government data tomorrow will show economic growth\nslowed, giving policy makers scope to loosen monetary policy.  China Shenhua Energy Co. (601088)  and China Coal Energy Co. advanced\nmore than 2 percent on expectations an earthquake in  Indonesia \nwill strain supplies from the world\u2019s largest exporter of\nthermal coal. Citic Securities Co. advanced to a nine-month high\nafter the stock-market regulator approved 11 more overseas\ninvestors to buy yuan-denominated securities. Hainan Airlines\nCo. rallied on a plan to receive assets from its parent.  The  Shanghai Composite Index (SHCOMP)  gained 41.94 points, or 1.8\npercent, to 2,350.86 at the close, its biggest advance since\nFeb. 8. The gauge climbed before data tomorrow that will\nprobably show the economy expanded 8.4 percent in the first\nquarter, the slowest pace since 2009, according to the median of\n41 economists\u2019 estimates compiled by Bloomberg. Industrial\nproduction and retail sales accelerated in March while spending\non fixed assets slowed, other government reports may show.  \u201cThe market is waiting for tomorrow\u2019s data, which may\ndefinitely show the economy is slowing down,\u201d said Wang Weijun,\na strategist at Zheshang Securities Co. in  Shanghai . \u201cThat may\njustify the government taking measures such as a cut in  interest\nrates  to boost growth.\u201d  Economic Slowdown  The  CSI 300 Index (SHSZ300)  rose 2 percent to 2,570.44. The  Bloomberg\nChina-US 55 Index (CH55BN) , the measure of the most-traded U.S.-listed\nChinese companies, added 1.8 percent in  New York  yesterday.  Thirty-day  volatility  in the Shanghai Composite was at 18.7\ntoday, the highest level since March 2. About 7.5 billion shares\nchanged hands in the gauge yesterday, 14 percent lower than the\ndaily average this year.  The Shanghai index has gained 6.9 percent this year on\nspeculation the government will cut lenders\u2019 reserve\nrequirements and borrowing costs to boost the economy. Stocks in\nthe gauge are valued at 9.9 times estimated earnings, compared\nwith a record low of 8.9 times on Jan. 6, according to weekly\n data  compiled by Bloomberg.  China  may cut interest rates in the \u201cimmediate future\u201d as\ninflation isn\u2019t too high to prevent further easing of monetary\npolicy,  Michael Kurtz , chief Asian equity strategist at Nomura\nHoldings Inc., wrote in a report dated yesterday. Reports this\nweek showed the inflation rate rose by a faster-than-estimated\n3.6 percent in March, while growth in imports and exports\nslowed.  Loan Data  Policy makers have cut the amount banks must keep in\nreserves twice since November to free up cash for lending, in a\nbid to insulate the world\u2019s second-largest economy from the\neffects of a global slowdown. Interest rates haven\u2019t been\nreduced since 2008.  New local-currency lending was 1.01 trillion yuan ($160.1\nbillion) in March, the People\u2019s Bank of China said on its\nwebsite after the market closed. That compared with the median\n797.5 billion yuan estimate in a Bloomberg News survey of 28\neconomists and 710.7 billion yuan the previous month. M2 money\nsupply climbed 13.4 last month, accelerating from a 13 percent\ngrowth in February, the central bank said.  Shenhua, the nation\u2019s largest coal producer, gained 2.3\npercent to 26.24 yuan. China Coal, the second largest, added 2.3\npercent to 9.19 yuan.  Datong Coal Industry Co. (601001) , the third\nbiggest, climbed 2.9 percent to 13.24 yuan.  The 8.6-magnitude quake in Indonesia hit 431 kilometers\n(268 miles) off the coast of the western province of Aceh, one\nof the nation\u2019s poorest provinces, where 170,000 people died or\nwent missing in a tsunami in 2004.  Brokerages Gain  China\u2019s Bohai-Rim Steam-Coal Price Index, which tracks\npower-station coal prices at six ports, rose 0.6 percent to 782\nyuan a metric ton yesterday compared with a week earlier,\naccording to the Qinhuangdao Seaborne Coal Market website.  China Hainan Rubber Industry Group Co. (601118) , the nation\u2019s sole\nlisted producer of natural rubber, jumped 6.5 percent to 7.19\nyuan on speculation the quake will disrupt supply from\nIndonesia, the world\u2019s second-largest producer of the commodity.  Citic Securities (600030)  led a gain among brokerages on\nexpectations they will benefit from the regulator\u2019s plan to\nexpand the qualified foreign institutional investor, or QFII,\nprogram. The stock climbed 4.1 percent to 12.99 yuan, its\nhighest close since July 15.  GF Securities Co., China\u2019s second-biggest listed brokerage,\ngained 3.9 percent to 30.84 yuan. Haitong Securities Co., the\nthird largest, advanced 4.4 percent to 10.22 yuan.  More QFIIs  JPMorgan Asset Management UK Ltd., American International\nAssurance Co. and nine other overseas investors were approved by\nthe China Securities Regulatory Commission to receive investment\nlicenses, according to data on the regulator\u2019s website today.  The regulator has approved 158 QFIIs so far, the data\nshowed. The CSRC said on April 4 it increased the QFII quotas to\n$80 billion from $30 billion.  Hainan Airlines (600221)  rose 2.2 percent to 4.60 yuan. The company\nwill receive stakes in Hong Kong Airlines and Grand China Air\nfrom the parent, the carrier said in a statement yesterday.  Chinese stocks  traded in the U.S. rallied the most in a\nmonth yesterday, led by solar companies.  The Bloomberg China-US Equity Index\u2019s advance yesterday was\nthe biggest since March 13. Solar company  Suntech Power Holdings\nCo. (STP)  posted the largest gain on the gauge as Satcon Technology\nCorp., a maker of inverters that connect renewable energy to\nelectricity grids, said bookings surged 130 percent in the first\nquarter from the last three months of 2011.  --Zhang Shidong.
