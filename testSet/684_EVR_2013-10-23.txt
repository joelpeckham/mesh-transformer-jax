Earnings excluding certain\nitems were 53 cents a share, surpassing the 43-cent  average\nestimate  of seven analysts surveyed by Bloomberg. Greenhill & Co., the independent advisory firm\nthat reported third-quarter results last week, said revenue\ncould increase this year from 2012 as that New York-based\ncompany boosts its share of fees.  \u201cOur productivity per partner has gone up fairly\nconsistently over the last three years in what has been\nessentially a flattish environment,\u201d Schlosstein, 62, said on a\nconference call after results were announced. \u201cGetting market\nshare improvement from productivity enhancement is an\nencouraging thing.\u201d  Evercore raised its quarterly dividend 14 percent to 25\ncents a share and increased the amount of shares it could\nrepurchase to as much as $250 million, according to the\nstatement.  Banking Revenue  Adjusted net revenue from  investment banking , which\nincludes fees from advising clients, climbed 26 percent to\n$161.3 million in the third quarter from a year earlier, and 30\npercent to $472 million in the first nine months.  During the quarter, Evercore\u2019s investment-banking unit\nearned advisory fees from 136 clients, compared with 147 a year\nearlier, according to the statement. The firm said it collected\nfees of $1 million or more from 31 clients, versus 30 in the\nthird quarter last year.  Evercore set aside $324.2 million for compensation costs in\nthe first nine months, or 59 percent of adjusted net revenue,\ncompared with $258.6 million, or 61 percent, in the same period\nlast year.  The company said it took a $2.7 million impairment charge\nin the period after a decline in the fair value of its 68\npercent stake in Evercore Pan Asset Management, according to the\nstatement.
