Soybean futures for January delivery rose 1.2 percent to\n$14.0025 a bushel on the  Chicago  Board of Trade, heading for the\nbiggest gain since Oct. 18. As of Nov. 16, the oilseed had\nfallen 23 percent since touching a record $17.89 in September.  Wheat futures for March delivery gained 0.6 percent to\n$8.5875 a bushel in Chicago after sixth straight declines, the\nlongest losing streak since September 2011.  The U.S. was the biggest shipper of all three commodities\nlast year. Corn is the biggest crop, valued at $76.5 billion in\n2011, followed by soybeans at $35.8 billion, government figures\nshow. Wheat is the fourth-largest at $14.4 billion, behind hay.
