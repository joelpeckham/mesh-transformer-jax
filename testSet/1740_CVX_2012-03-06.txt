The body of the article is as follows:\n    \nBG Group Plc (BG/) , the U.K.\u2019s third-\nlargest oil and natural gas producer, is seeking investors for\nits Australian gas project, said three people with knowledge of\nthe matter.  The company\u2019s options include selling a stake of about 20\npercent in Australian unit QGC Pty Ltd. or accepting an\ninvestment in its liquefied natural gas plant in the country,\nsaid the people, declining to be identified as the process is\nprivate. A deal may be valued at $1.5 billion to $2 billion, one\nof the people said. BG\u2019s advisers have approached potential\nbuyers in  China  and  Japan , the person said.  BG said Feb. 9 it will sell $5 billion of assets in the\nnext two years to maintain investment and production growth at\nprojects in Brazil, the North Sea and Australia. The Reading,\nU.K.-based company plans to invest $22 billion in projects this\nyear and next.  \u201cWe keep all assets in our global portfolio under review\nand all options are open,\u201d said  Neil Burrows , a spokesman for\nBG, in an interview. He declined to comment on the plan to seek\ninvestors for BG\u2019s Australian assets.  BG plans to build two processing units at its plant near\nCurtis Island in Queensland state, with a combined capacity of\n8.5 million metric tons of LNG a year.  The company said in November it will proceed with the $15\nbillion LNG venture, which is being built by its Australian unit\nand will convert coal-seam gas to LNG for shipment to overseas\nmarkets. China National Offshore Oil Corp. will have a 10\npercent stake in the first LNG processing unit, while Tokyo Gas\nCo. will have a 2.5 percent interest in the second one, BG said\nin November.  LNG Competition  BG,  Chevron Corp., (CVX)  Royal Dutch Shell Plc and ConocoPhillips\nare among energy companies investing about A$200 billion ($214\nbillion) in LNG projects in Australia to tap Asian demand for\ncleaner-burning fuel, according to data from the Australian\nPetroleum Production & Exploration Association.  Australian gas producer Santos Ltd. plans to produce 7.2\nmillion tons of LNG a year from two units at its venture in\nGladstone, a coastal town more than 300 kilometers north of\nBrisbane.  BG\u2019s Curtis Island plant will be built over the next four\nyears and is due to start fuel shipments in 2014, the company\nsaid Nov. 1. It has reached agreements to sell LNG to customers\nin China, Japan,  Singapore  and  Chile  and will also provide gas\nto markets in eastern Australia.  Last month, BG raised its gas resource estimate in\nAustralia to 25 trillion cubic feet, from an earlier forecast of\n21 trillion.  BG shares slipped 2.9 percent to 1472 pence ($23.25) in\nafternoon trading in  London  on Tuesday.
