Islamic bonds\nreturned 6 percent so far this year, according to the\n HSBC/NASDAQ Dubai Listed US Dollar Sukuk Index , while regular\ndebt in developing markets gained 5.45 percent, JPMorgan Chase &\nCo.\u2019s  EMBI Global Diversified Index  shows.  \n The World Bank predicted this month that economic growth in\nthe Middle East and North Africa will accelerate to 4 percent in\n2010 from an estimated 3.2 percent growth last year as oil\nprices rebound. The difference over\nsimilar-maturity U.S. Treasuries has narrowed five basis points\nto 175 since then.  \n The spread between the average yield for emerging-market\nsukuk and the London interbank offered rate narrowed 37 basis\npoints, or 0.37 percentage point, to 431 so far this year,\naccording to the  HSBC/NASDAQ Dubai US Dollar Sukuk Index .  \n The  difference  between the average developing-nation yield\nand Libor narrowed 49 basis points to 586 basis points, based on\nthe EMBI+ index from JPMorgan Chase & Co. That is 156 basis\npoints more than the average spread for sukuk.  \n Pakistan Sukuk  \n Transactions in the $1 trillion Islamic finance industry\nare based on the exchange of assets rather than interest to\ncomply with Shariah principles.
