European Earnings  More than 73 percent of the 294 companies in the Standard &\nPoor\u2019s 500 Index that have reported results since Jan. 10 topped\nearnings-per-share projections, according to Bloomberg data. In  Europe , 56 percent beat forecasts, the data show. Companies from\nCredit Suisse Group AG to ArcelorMittal are scheduled to report\nresults this week. Adidas jumped 3.6 percent to 47.69 euros. Chief Executive\nOfficer Herbert Hainer said the German sporting-goods maker will\ngrow significantly this year after a strong 2010, DAF Deutsches\nAnleger Fernsehen reported, citing an interview. Adidas\u2019s sales\nin  China  will exceed 1 billion euros this year, Hainer said. The company\u2019s full-year net income fell to 353 million\nfrancs from 389 million francs a year earlier as clients\nreassessed the benefits of their cross-border accounts.  Umeco jumped 4.9 percent to 483 pence after saying it is in\n\u201cnon-exclusive discussions\u201d that may lead to the sale of its\nPattonair supply-chain unit.
