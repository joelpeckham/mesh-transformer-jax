The body of the article is as follows:\n    \nChina\u2019s stocks  rose as consumer\ncompanies extended the biggest weekly rally among industry\ngroups on speculation they will benefit most from government\nefforts to focus on domestic demand to support economic growth.  Liquor makers  Kweichow Moutai Co. (600519)  and Wuliangye Yibin Co.\nadvanced at least 3.1 percent after Shenyin & Wanguo Securities\nCo. recommended buying consumer stocks. Jiangling Motors Corp.,\nthe commercial vehicle partner with Ford Motor Co. gained 2.6\npercent after net income rose 9.3 percent last year. Shanghai\nPudong Development Bank, the Chinese partner of Citigroup Inc.,\nadded 0.7 percent after profit jumped 42 percent in 2011.  \u201cEarnings don\u2019t look bad and valuations are attractive,\u201d\nsaid Wu Kan, a Shanghai-based  fund manager  at Dazhong Insurance\nCo., which oversees $285 million. \u201cEven though the economy is\nstill slowing, the market expects it to find a bottom by the end\nof the first quarter.\u201d  The  Shanghai Composite Index (SHCOMP)  climbed 30.96 points, or 1.3\npercent, to 2,404.74 at the close. The  CSI 300 Index (SHSZ300)  rose 1.5\npercent to 2,623.52. The  Bloomberg China-US 55 Index (CH55BN) , the\nmeasure of the most-traded U.S.-listed Chinese companies, added\n0.6 percent in New York yesterday.  About 16.6 billion shares changed hands in the Shanghai\nComposite yesterday, or 33 percent higher than the daily average\nthis year. Thirty-day  volatility  on the gauge was at 16.42,\nclose to its highest level in almost two weeks.  Weekly Loss  The Shanghai Composite retreated 1.4 percent this week, the\nbiggest weekly loss since January, after Premier  Wen Jiabao  said\n home prices  are still far from reasonable levels. The slump\npared this year\u2019s gains to 9.3 percent in 2012. Stocks in the\nindex trade at 10 times estimated profit, compared with a record\nlow of 8.9 times on Jan. 6,  weekly data  compiled by Bloomberg\nshowed.  A measure of consumer staples stocks in the CSI 300 gained\n3.4 percent, the most among the 10 industry groups today and\nthis week. Kweichow Moutai,  China \u2019s biggest producer of baijiu\nliquor by market value, rose 4.4 percent to 207.59 yuan, the\nbiggest gain since August. Wuliangye, the second largest, added\n3.1 percent to 37.35 yuan. Luzhou Laojiao Co., a spirits\nproducer in Sichuan province, jumped 4.8 percent to 45.47 yuan.  Consumer stocks may be in favor as property-related shares\nare getting riskier and small-capitalization equities face\nearnings risk, Ling Peng, a strategist at Shenyin & Wanguo,\nwrote in a report.  A relaxation of curbs on the  property market  would lead to\n\u201cchaos,\u201d Wen said at a March 14 press conference in Beijing.\nChina trimmed the nation\u2019s economic growth target to 7.5 percent\nfrom an 8 percent goal in place since 2005, a signal that\nleaders are seeking to shift toward expansion driven more by\ndomestic consumption instead of exports.  Hard Landing Risk  The government also highlighted plans to endorse higher\nminimum wages during this month\u2019s meeting of the National\nPeople\u2019s Congress to reduce the risk of a slowdown turning into\na so-called hard landing.  \u201cIf you look at the Chinese data, you should stop debating\nabout a hard landing,\u201d  Adrian Mowat , JPMorgan Chase & Co.\u2019s\nchief Asian and emerging-market strategist, said at a conference\nin  Singapore  on March 14. \u201cChina is in a hard landing. Car\nsales are down, cement production is down, steel production is\ndown, construction stocks are down. It\u2019s not a debate anymore,\nit\u2019s a fact.\u201d  Earnings Growth  Listed companies have started to announce annual earnings\nreports and will finish before the end of April. They may post\nthe slowest earnings growth since 2008, when profits dropped 11\npercent amid the global financial crisis, according to  data \ncompiled by Bloomberg.  Two hundred and sixty-three companies in the Shanghai\nComposite have released annual earnings. They posted profit\ngrowth of 14 percent on average, trailing analyst estimates by\n6.8 percent, Bloomberg data showed. That compared with an\nincrease of 38 percent in the previous year.  Jiangling Motors gained 2.6 percent to 22.52 yuan after\nreporting net income of 1.87 billion yuan last year. Xinzhi\nMotor Co. jumped 40.6 percent to 22.50 yuan on its first day of\ntrading in Shenzhen. Pudong Bank rose 0.7 percent to 9.25 yuan.  China will allow exchange-traded funds of  Hong Kong  shares\nto list on mainland exchanges \u201csoon,\u201d K.C. Chan, Hong Kong\u2019s\nSecretary for Financial Services and the Treasury, said in an\ninterview in  New York  yesterday.  The process of getting Hong Kong stocks to trade on the\nShanghai and Shenzhen exchanges as ETFs is \u201calmost near\ncompletion,\u201d Chan said. \u201cThe final step would be for the\nregulators in China\u201d to review companies\u2019 applications to start\nthe funds, he said.  The iShares FTSE China 25 Index Fund, the biggest Chinese\nexchange-traded fund in the U.S., rose 0.5 percent to $38.86\nyesterday, climbing for the second day this week.  --Zhang Shidong.
