The body of the article is as follows:\n    \nRussian (INDEXCF)  equities dropped for a\nsecond day as oil and gas stocks led declines after the World\nBank cut its global growth forecast and concern U.S. debt limits\nwill derail a recovery pared appetite for riskier assets.  The Micex Index lost less than 0.1 percent to 1,514.86 by\nthe close in Moscow after falling as much as 0.5 percent\nearlier. Of the 50 stocks in the gauge, 24 climbed and 26\nretreated. OAO Novatek,  Russia \u2019s largest independent natural gas\nproducer, dropped 2.7 percent. OAO Rosneft, the nation\u2019s biggest\noil producer, fell 1.3 percent.  Oil and gas  shares sank 0.6\npercent on average, the most of nine industry groups.  Emerging-market stocks fell to a two-week low after the\nWorld Bank yesterday cut its global growth forecast for this\nyear as austerity measures, high unemployment and low business\nconfidence weigh on economies. With as little as a month until\nthe U.S. runs out of money to pay its bills, President  Barack Obama  warned Republicans in Congress not to use the need for a\ndebt-limit increase to force through new spending cuts.  \u201cInvestors are concerned about the U.S. debt ceiling, they\nrealize that the limit can\u2019t continue to be raised because this\neats up the nation\u2019s economic future,\u201d Sergey Kucherenko, who\nmanages about $50 million in Russian equities at Nomos Bank in\nMoscow, said by phone. \u201cSince the American economy is the\nlargest, Russian investors are keen to see the debt issue\nresolved.\u201d  The amount of shares traded on the Micex was 49 percent\nabove the 30-day average, data compiled by Bloomberg show. OAO\nGazprom, Russia\u2019s largest company and natural gas export\nmonopoly, dropped 1.1 percent to 146.33 rubles. The stock has a\n14.8 percent  weighting  on the index.  Rates Decision  Russia yesterday left borrowing costs unchanged this month\nand dropped a phrase that money-market rates are acceptable for\nthe \u201cnearest future,\u201d a sign interest rates may be cut as\nearly as next month to bolster growth.  The Washington-based bank  World Bank  yesterday projected\nthe world economy will expand 2.4 percent, down from a June\nforecast of 3 percent, after growing 2.3 percent in 2012. It\nhalved its forecast for  Japan , cut the U.S. projection by 0.5\npercentage point and predicted a second year of contraction in\nthe euro region. It also lowered projections for  emerging\nmarkets  led by  Brazil ,  India  and  Mexico .  OAO Raspadskaya, a coal producer, rose as much as 1.8\npercent before closing up 1.2 percent at 69.12 rubles, the\nhighest level since Oct. 18.  Evraz Plc (EVR) , the Russian steelmaker\npart-owned by billionaire  Roman Abramovich , said today it has\ncompleted the acquisition of Raspadskaya, according to a\nstatement. Evraz added 0.7 percent to 287.30 pence in  London .  Magnit, MTS  OAO Magnit, the nation\u2019s biggest food retailer by market\nvalue, surged 4.6 percent to 5,370.60 rubles, the highest level\nsince July 2006. Merill Lynch raised Magnit\u2019s global\ndepositary receipts to buy, citing \u201cstronger\u201d margin\nassumptions. OAO Mobile TeleSystems declined as much as 0.9 percent\nbefore closing up 0.2 percent at 247.75 rubles. Russia\u2019s largest\ntelecoms operator said its Uzbek unit filed for bankruptcy after\nbeing unable to pay a $600 million fine to the country\u2019s\nauthorities.  The  Market Vectors Russia ETF (RSX) , the largest dedicated\nRussian exchange-traded fund, slid 0.2 percent to $29.83.
