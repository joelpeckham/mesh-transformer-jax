The body of the article is as follows:\n    \nChina\u2019s stocks  fell for a second day\nas speculation corporate earnings growth will slow because of\nthe decelerating economy overshadowed a government report\nshowing inflation eased.  Chongqing Department Store (600729)  Co. dropped the most since\nNovember 2008 after profit trailed analysts\u2019 estimates.\nNortheast Securities Co. led a decline for brokerages after it\nposted a loss last year. Changzhou Eging Photovoltaic Technology\nCo. and Shanghai Chaori Solar Energy Science & Technology Co.\njumped more than 3 percent after a government body said China\nplans to double solar capacity this year.  \u201cThe risks of an economic slowdown on earnings growth have\nyet to be fully known, making the market volatile,\u201d said  Zhang Ling , general manager at Shanghai River Fund Management Co.\n\u201cSlowing inflation is positive news and that allows the\ngovernment to further relax tightening policies and add more\nliquidity to the economy and the stock market.\u201d  The  Shanghai Composite Index (SHCOMP)  slipped 1.04 point, or 0.1\npercent, to 2,275.01 at the close, erasing a gain of as much as\n0.8 percent. About six stocks fell every five that rose on the\nmeasure. The  CSI 300 Index  lost less than 0.1 percent to\n2,435.22. The  Bloomberg China-US 55 Index , the measure of the\nmost-traded U.S.-listed Chinese companies, retreated 0.2 percent\nyesterday in  New York .  Stocks rose earlier after a report from the statistics\nbureau showed inflation moderated for a fifth straight month in\nDecember. Consumer prices rose 4.1 percent, compared with the\nmedian estimate of 4 percent in a Bloomberg News survey of 26\neconomists and 4.2 percent in November. Producer prices\nincreased 1.7 percent, the smallest gain in two years, it said.  Inflation, GDP Data  While lower inflation should provide officials with greater\nscope to ease policy in the months ahead, full-scale policy\nreversal isn\u2019t on the agenda as senior officials favor\n\u201cprudent\u201d monetary policy,  Brian Jackson , strategist at Royal\nBank of Canada, said in a report.  China\u2019s economic growth may have slowed to 8.7 percent from\na year earlier in the fourth quarter of 2011, according the\nmedian  estimate  of 24 economists in a Bloomberg News survey. The\ndata are scheduled to be released Jan. 17. UBS AG estimates the\npace of expansion may slow to 7.7 percent this quarter.  Chongqing Department Store plunged 8.7 percent to 28.61\nyuan. Net income increased 14 percent from a year earlier to\n599.3 million yuan in 2011, the company said in a preliminary\nstatement yesterday. That  missed  the median estimate of 678\nmillion yuan in a survey by Bloomberg News of five analysts.\nFourth-quarter revenue rose 8.5 percent, lagging behind the\n\u201ctwo-digit\u201d growth of rivals, said Qian Bing and Guo Haiyan,\nanalysts at  China  International Capital Corp.  Slowing Earnings  Dashang Group Co., a retailer based in the northeast\nLiaoning province, slid 5.1 percent to 29.48 yuan. Beijing\nWangfujing Department Store (Group) Co. fell 3.8 percent to\n31.22 yuan.  Northeast Securities led declines for brokerages,\nretreating 0.9 percent to 13.06 yuan after posting a loss of\n151.9 million yuan last year. China Everbright Securities Co., a\nunit of the nation\u2019s largest state-owned investment group, slid\n0.7 percent to 10.42 yuan after it reported a loss of 75 million\nyuan in December.  Chinese listed companies have started to announce annual\nearnings reports and will finish before the end of April. Nine\ncompanies in the Shanghai Composite have released annual profits\nfor 2011 so far, with an average gain of 29 percent, according\nto  data  compiled by Bloomberg. That compared with an increase of\n38 percent in the previous year.  Shanghai Index  Earnings growth for the 300 companies in the CSI 300 may\nslow to 5 percent compared with the consensus estimate of 19\npercent, Manop Sangiambut, head of China A-share research at\nCLSA Asia-Pacific Markets, said in a report dated Jan. 10.  The Shanghai Composite has advanced 3.4 percent this year\non speculation the government will add to a Nov. 30 cut in\nlenders\u2019 reserve requirements ratio to spur more lending to\nsmall companies hurt by a cash crunch. A central bank report\nreleased over the weekend showed new lending and money supply\nexceeded estimates in December. Import growth fell to a two-year\nlow in December, the customs office said this week.  The Shanghai gauge trades at 9.3 times estimated earnings,\nup from a record low of 8.9 times reached on Jan. 6, according\nto weekly  data  compiled by Bloomberg. The index plunged 22\npercent last year after the central bank raised  interest rates \nand reserve requirement ratios to combat inflation.  Eging Photovoltaic jumped 3.2 percent to 20.57 yuan,\nbringing its gain to 14 percent this week. Chaori Solar advanced\n4.3 percent to 14.03 yuan after saying it will supply 150\nmegawatts of modules this year to China Solar Gmbh.  The head of China\u2019s National Energy Administration Liu Tienan said yesterday that the country plans to double the\namount of solar capacity in operation by installing 3 gigawatts\nthis year.  The price for polysilicon, the raw material used to make\nmost solar panels, rose 3 percent last week, the most in eight\nmonths, Bloomberg New Energy Finance data showed.  --Zhang Shidong.
