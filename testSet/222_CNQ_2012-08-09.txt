Canadian Natural  gained  4.8 percent to C$30.99 at 1:13 p.m.\nin Toronto, the highest intraday price since May 30. The company\nhad the third-biggest increase among the 64 members of the\nStandard & Poor\u2019s/Toronto Stock Exchange Energy Index.  Excluding one-time items, second-quarter earnings were 55\ncents, beating the 53-cent average of 15  analysts\u2019 estimates \ncompiled by Bloomberg.  Net income  dropped to C$753 million ($758\nmillion), or 68 cents a share, from C$929 million, or 84 cents,\na year earlier, the Calgary-based company said in a statement\ntoday.  Lower-than-expected royalties and cash taxes contributed to\nthe beat, Phil Skolnick, a New York-based research analyst with\nCanaccord Genuity Corp., said in a note to clients today.  Sales rose 12 percent to C$4.19 billion from C$3.73 billion\na year earlier. Canadian Natural produced a record amount of oil\nand natural gas during the quarter, boosting output 22 percent\nto the equivalent of 679,607 barrels a day. Lower prices for the\nfuels contributed to the drop in income for the quarter, the\ncompany said.  \u201cCommodity prices continue to be volatile with a mixed\noutlook,\u201d Vice Chairman John Langille said on a conference call\nwith analysts today. The company is \u201cbullish\u201d on heavy oil\nwhile it sees continued low prices in gas markets, President\n Steve Laut  said on the call.  Cutting Spending  In response, Canadian Natural is cutting its 2012 capital\nspending plan by C$680 million, or 5 percent, to C$4.8 billion.\nMost of the spending reductions will be at its Horizon oil-sands\nplant in  Alberta  and in North American gas operations, the\ncompany said in the release.  Canadian Natural narrowed its forecast for oil and natural\ngas liquids production this year to 454,000 to 474,000 barrels a\nday from 440,000 to 480,000. It reduced its gas output estimate\nto as much as 1.235 billion cubic feet of gas a day, from May 3\nguidance of as much as 1.26 billion cubic feet.  The company cut its 2012 gas drilling program in half\nduring the second quarter, reducing the number of wells drilled\nto 35 from 71. Canadian Natural has also shut in 20 million\ncubic feet of gas a day this year, Laut said.  The stock, which has declined 19 percent this year, has 20\nbuy and five hold  ratings  from analysts.  Suncor Energy Inc. (SU)  and  Imperial Oil Ltd. (IMO)  are the largest\nCanadian crude companies.
