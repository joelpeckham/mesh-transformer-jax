Bernanke\nsaid on Oct. 4 that the central bank aided the U.S. economy\nthrough its $1.75 trillion purchases of mortgage debt and\nTreasuries that ended in March 2010 and that further buying\nwould likely help more.  Nine of the 10 most-actively-traded currencies in Asia\noutside Japan have gained against the dollar this year, led by\nthe Thai baht and Malaysia\u2019s ringgit. Overseas funds have poured\nmoney into the region as economic growth outpaces the U.S. and\nEurope.  Taiwan Economy  Asia\u2019s developing economies will expand 9.2 percent in\n2010, compared with growth of 2.6 percent in advanced countries,\nthe International Monetary Fund forecast in July. Foreign\ninvestors bought more than $35 billion of stocks in India, South\nKorea and Taiwan than they sold this year, according to exchange\ndata. The currency has gained 0.6 percent since the central bank\non Sept. 30 raised its  policy rate  for the second time this year\nto 1.5 percent from 1.375 percent. A government report tomorrow\nmay show exports increased for an 11th straight month in\nSeptember. Overseas sales, which account for more than two-\nthirds of Taiwan\u2019s economy, climbed 26.6 percent from a year\nearlier, the same pace as in August, according to the median\nforecast in a Bloomberg survey.
